QRK is an AI research lab from Abuja, Nigeria. We work on model representation, learned tokenization, and retrieval during reasoning.

We're a small team of researchers and engineers working across machine learning, natural language processing, data engineering, and software infrastructure.

Our current public work starts with DABE, a learned tokenizer-autoencoder, plus notes and essays around memory, retrieval, and how models represent text.

This work is driven by a simple interest:

### HOW MODELS HOLD DETAIL

Language models are very good at broad patterns, but detail still matters: names, syntax, citations, rare phrases, local context, and the small pieces of text that change the meaning of an answer.

DABE looks at that question through tokenization. Thought injection looks at it through retrieval. Both are ways of asking how a model should decide what to remember and when to ask for more context.

We care about mechanisms that are small enough to study and useful enough to keep exploring.

### HOW DO WE WANT TO DO IT?

As a small team in Abuja, we want to do careful work and explain it clearly.

1. **Study mechanisms**: Start with specific questions about representation, retrieval, and memory.
2. **Write plainly**: Make the technical work easier to inspect.
3. **Publish the trail**: Share papers, notes, and experiments as the work develops.

Nothing is set in stone. We honestly don't know what we are doing, but we want to try.
