We started QRK because we wanted a small place to work on AI research from Abuja.

The first questions we are exploring are about representation, retrieval, and memory: how models hold detail, how they recover context, and where tokenization can become more adaptive.

## The Problem We See

Models can do a lot now. They can write code, summarize documents, and help people move faster through complicated work. But there are still many small mechanisms inside these systems that deserve closer study.

That is the part that interests us.

We want to ask focused questions, run experiments, and explain the work in a way other people can follow.

## What We Are Working On

QRK is a small AI research lab. Our approach is simple:

1. **Study mechanisms**: Start with specific questions about model behavior.

2. **Write clearly**: Pair technical work with plain explanations.

3. **Publish the trail**: Share papers, notes, and experiments as the work develops.

## Our Research Focus

Our current work spans:

- **DABE**: A learned tokenizer-autoencoder that keeps text in fixed chunks, forms a coarse gist, and uses sparse repair where exact reconstruction is difficult.
- **Thought injection notes**: Writing on retrieval during model reasoning.
- **Research explanations**: Plain-language posts that connect each technical result to the question behind it.

Some work is a manuscript. Some is a note. Both belong here.

## Why Abuja?

We are based in Abuja, Nigeria. That matters to how we see the work and the kind of research culture we want to help build.

## What Comes Next

We are just getting started. Over the coming months, we will be sharing:

- Research papers and preprints
- Technical deep dives into our methods
- Notes on what worked, what failed, and what remains unresolved

We do not have all the answers. We are figuring this out as we go.
